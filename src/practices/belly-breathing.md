## What to do

1. Find a comfortable position. Sit or lie down.
2. Rest one hand on your chest and the other on your belly.
3. Breathe in slowly through your nose for four counts. The hand on your belly should rise more than the hand on your chest.
4. Breathe out gently through your mouth or nose for six counts. Let your belly fall.
5. Repeat for twelve cycles, about two minutes.

If counting feels like work, watch the breathing figure below and ignore the numbers.

## Why it works

Slow diaphragmatic breathing at about six breaths per minute calms the part of the nervous system that runs the fight-or-flight response. The longer exhale relative to inhale does most of the work. It slows the heart rate and increases heart rate variability, a measure of how flexibly the body recovers from stress.
