## What to do

1. Sit comfortably with the spine tall.
2. Rest the tip of your tongue against the ridge of tissue behind your upper front teeth and keep it there throughout the practice.
3. Exhale completely through your mouth around your tongue, making a soft whoosh sound.
4. Close your mouth and breathe in quietly through your nose for a count of four.
5. Hold your breath for a count of seven.
6. Exhale through your mouth around your tongue for a count of eight, making the whoosh sound.
7. Repeat for four cycles.

Andrew Weil, who popularized this protocol, suggests starting with four cycles and building up to eight over time. The total duration of one cycle is about nineteen seconds.

## Why it works

The pattern is a slow-paced breath with an extended exhale (twice the inhale duration) and a sustained hold at the top. The slow overall rate and the longer exhale activate the parasympathetic branch of the nervous system. The hold gives the body time to fully oxygenate before the long release.

The principles fit within the broader slow-breathing literature. Smaller direct studies on the 4-7-8 protocol have shown short-term effects on heart rate variability, blood pressure, and self-reported anxiety. The pattern is widely taught for acute stress, falling asleep, and managing strong emotions.
