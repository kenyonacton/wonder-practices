## What this is

The Vagus Reset is a short sequence that engages the vagus nerve from four different angles in succession. Each step is researched on its own (links in the steps below); pulling them together adds reliability for moments when a single brief practice isn't enough to shift the state.

Total time is about eight minutes.

## When to use it

Use this routine when you have eight uninterrupted minutes and want a deeper reset than a single short practice provides. Good fits include after a hard meeting, before sleep, during a stressful afternoon when one short practice isn't holding, or any time the nervous system feels stuck in high alert.

## Why this sequence

Each step targets a different vagal pathway:

- **Belly breathing** activates the parasympathetic branch through the slow, extended-exhale pathway.
- **Humming** adds vagal vibration on top of the slow exhale; nasal cavity, throat, and chest all vibrate the vagus directly.
- **Cool face reset** engages the mammalian dive reflex for a fast cardiac slowdown that breath alone takes longer to produce.
- **Relaxation response** consolidates the shift, letting the nervous system settle into the new lower-arousal baseline for a longer span.

The cold step is the most intense. If cold isn't available or appropriate (cardiovascular conditions, pregnancy, history of fainting), skip it; the other three steps still produce a meaningful effect on their own.
